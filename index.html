Landmarks in New York Harbor

Governors Island is located in New York Harbor between Lower Manhattan and the Brooklyn waterfront. The entire island is made up of 172-acres of land. During the American Revolutionary War, Governors Island was used by the military. In 1996, the New York City Landmarks Preservation Commission created the Governors Island historic district. Today, the island is open to the public and many of the historic buildings remain in place.
